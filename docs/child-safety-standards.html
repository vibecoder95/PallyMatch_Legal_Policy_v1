Child Safety Standards

Last Updated: January 2025

PallyMatch is committed to protecting children and preventing child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) on our platform.

1. Our Commitment

PallyMatch takes the safety of children seriously and is committed to maintaining a platform free of child sexual abuse and exploitation. We have zero tolerance for any content, behavior, or activity that harms children or exploits minors in any way.

2. Standards Against Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE)

PallyMatch strictly prohibits:

  • Any form of child sexual abuse material (CSAM)
  • Grooming, solicitation, or exploitation of minors
  • Sharing, creating, or distributing content that depicts or describes sexual abuse of children
  • Any communication or behavior that targets or exploits individuals under 18 years of age
  • Attempts to contact, communicate with, or meet with minors for inappropriate purposes
  • Misrepresentation of age to access or interact with minors
  • Any activity that violates child protection laws

Violations of these standards will result in:

  • Immediate and permanent account termination
  • Reporting to law enforcement authorities
  • Cooperation with legal investigations
  • Preservation of evidence for legal proceedings

3. Age Verification and Restrictions

PallyMatch is designed exclusively for users 18 years and older:

  • All users must confirm they are at least 18 years old during registration
  • We do not knowingly allow users under 18 to access our platform
  • If we discover a user is under 18, their account will be immediately terminated
  • We reserve the right to request age verification documentation when necessary

4. Addressing Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM)

4.1 Detection and Reporting

PallyMatch has implemented multiple measures to detect and address CSAM:

  • Automated content scanning and moderation systems
  • User reporting mechanisms for suspected CSAM or CSAE
  • 24/7 monitoring and review of reported content
  • Regular audits of our safety systems

4.2 Response Procedures

When CSAM or CSAE is detected or reported:

  • Content is immediately removed from our platform
  • All relevant accounts are permanently banned
  • We report to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) and relevant law enforcement
  • We preserve all evidence for law enforcement investigations
  • We cooperate fully with legal authorities

4.3 Law Enforcement Cooperation

PallyMatch fully cooperates with law enforcement agencies:

  • We respond promptly to legal requests and subpoenas
  • We preserve user data and communications when required by law
  • We provide information necessary for investigations
  • We comply with all applicable child protection laws and regulations

5. User Reporting Mechanism

Users can report suspected CSAM or CSAE through multiple channels:

  • In-App Reporting: Use the report feature in any chat conversation
  • Email: Send reports to our child safety contact (see below)
  • Emergency: If you believe a child is in immediate danger, contact local law enforcement immediately

When reporting, please provide:

  • Details of the suspected violation
  • User ID or username of the reported individual
  • Chat ID or message content (if applicable)
  • Any relevant screenshots or evidence
  • Timestamp of the incident

6. Compliance with Child Safety Laws

PallyMatch complies with all applicable child safety laws and regulations, including but not limited to:

  • Protection of Children from Sexual Predators Act
  • Child Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)
  • International child protection laws and regulations
  • Requirements of the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC)
  • All local, state, and federal laws regarding child protection

8. Training and Awareness

PallyMatch is committed to ongoing education and improvement:

  • Our team receives regular training on child safety issues
  • We stay updated on best practices for combating online child exploitation
  • We participate in industry initiatives to improve child safety online
  • We follow guidance from organizations like the Tech Coalition

9. Transparency and Accountability

PallyMatch is committed to transparency in our child safety efforts:

  • We publish these standards publicly
  • We regularly review and update our policies
  • We maintain records of our safety measures and responses
  • We work with child safety organizations and experts

11. Updates to These Standards

We may update these Child Safety Standards periodically to reflect changes in laws, technology, or best practices. Significant changes will be communicated to users, and the "Last Updated" date will be revised accordingly.
